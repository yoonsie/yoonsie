Ruminations and remnants / how we limit ourselves

We hold ourselves back with limiting beliefs. What outdated beliefs are holding you back? Here's a guide to find out what they are and methods to correct any invalid beliefs

Step 1: Identify

  • Identity-based: I'm not a [type of] person who can...
    • do mental math well
    • draw
    • be consistent with anything
  • Capacity-based: I don't have the time/skills/resources/conditions to...
    • exercise
    • cook
    • throw a themed party
  • Merit-based: I don't deserve to X, until Y [happens]
    • ask for help at work; I prove my worth
    • love my body; I lose weight
    • be happy; I get a degree/job/house
  • Outcome-based: If X, then that means Y
    • my project isn't perfect; I'm incompetent
    • I don't reach my goals by a certain age; I'm behind in life
    • I don't work on [project]; I am wasting my life
  • Fear-based: If X, then Y [will happen]...
    • ride a plane; a crash
    • I move; I will have no friends
    • things start going well; something bad will happen

Step 2: Analyze

  • Why Can't I? Framework: Keep asking yourself "Why" until you understand why you think you can't accomplish something. Try to objectively examine and reassess if this core belief is still or ever was valid.
    • Example original self-limiting belief: "I can't draw"
    • Why can't I?: I've always been bad
    • Why?: I have poor hand-eye coordination
    • Is this a valid claim (has this always been true in all contexts? Will it always definitively be the case considering the brain is plastic?): No, I can practice and improve my hand-eye coordination
  • Identify Thinking Traps: Review and examine if your belief falls into any of these common thinking traps
    • All-or-nothing/perfectionistic thinking: If I don't do this perfectly, I've failed; I can't do X, until Y, Z conditions are met.
    • Catastrophizing: If I fail at this, everyone will think I'm incompetent.
    • Over-generalizing: If I fail at this, that means I am a failure and will fail at Y and Z.
    • Mind-reading: People will think I'm annoying or too ambitious if I try to get promoted.
