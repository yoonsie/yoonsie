Ruminations and remnants / how to make friends as an adult

High-level steps

  1. do a social version of a hobby/interests that you have. it doesn't matter if it's offline or online, but i think you'll find more success offline
  2. show up consistently
  3. talk to people
    • ideas: ask them if they've been here before, how they found out about it, what motivated them to join, what they like about it, etc.
    • engage with familiar faces
  4. after vetting someone as friend potential, invite them to something outside of the shared event based on a connector
    • this connector can be anything, but it has to be something:
      • invite them to go with you to an event related to your shared hobby
      • invite them to go with you to your other hobby events if they expressed interest
      • if you both work from home, ask them to cowork together at your favorite cafe, or get lunch together
      • if they expressed interest in something (like food), ask them to grab a meal sometime
  5. if the initial friend date goes well, invite them continuously

Resources available in most cities

  • your local library
    • if you live in a transient metropolitan area (like me), these are great since you know they're [likely] a local, too. and free!
  • your local community center
    • again, more likely to meet locals
  • independent book shops
    • these are great because they have book clubs, book talks, book swaps, and other events
  • hobby-related stores for social events
    • bike shops or co-ops that often have social events for riding togheter
    • sewing shops
    • running shoe stores that have running clubs
    • etc
  • volunteer organizations
  • instagram accounts that promote events in your local city
  • meetups.com
